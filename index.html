I design AI systems that can critique their own reasoning before humans need to.

Hi, I'm Mandar — a machine learning engineer and researcher focused on building trustworthy and explainable AI systems for large-scale decision platforms.

My work sits at the intersection of production-scale machine learning and research on reasoning-based AI systems. I currently work on LLM-powered ranking systems that support large-scale content and advertising platforms serving billions of daily impressions.

Previously, I built recommendation and speech systems for millions of children at Epic!, demand forecasting models for Levi Strauss, and traffic simulation environments for autonomous vehicles at NIO.

My research explores a central question:

How can AI systems critique their own reasoning before humans need to?

I study methods such as self-questioning agents, debate-based reasoning, and reinforcement learning from model critique to improve the reliability and transparency of language models.

I also advise (pro bono) on AI strategy for the Western Cape Government in South Africa, helping design a multi-agent AI platform that transforms large collections of government documents into accessible knowledge systems. The platform is designed to scale from internal government use (~10K users) to potential public-facing services serving over 100K citizens.

In 2026 I received the Global Recognition Award for contributions to AI systems deployed across public-sector and large-scale consumer platforms.

My work has also been featured in Forbes Scotland and Forbes Liechtenstein , highlighting the role of explainable AI systems in public-sector and global technology environments.

When I'm not building AI systems, you'll probably find me playing games, reading manga, or diving into mythology and ancient epics.
