Hi, I'm Mandar.

I'm an ML Engineer at Meta working on LLaMA-based ranking systems serving billions of daily impressions. Before that: recommendation and speech systems for millions of kids at Epic!, demand forecasting at Levi's, and traffic simulation for autonomous vehicles at NIO.

My research interest is one question: how do we build AI systems that can explain their own reasoning before we trust them in production?

I recently published Counterfactual Self-Questioning (CSQ) — a framework where a single LLM generates counterfactual critiques of its own reasoning as a policy optimization signal, removing the need for external reward models or multiple agents. It integrates with GRPO and achieves +6.7 to +12.4 points over chain-of-thought on GSM8K, MATH, and Minerva-style benchmarks.

I'm also advising (pro bono) on AI strategy for the Western Cape Government in South Africa — scaling from ~10K internal users to 100K+ public-facing citizens. The platform was accepted by the Infrastructure Department and more departments are now in discussions.

In 2026 I received the Global Recognition Award for contributions to AI systems in public-sector and consumer-scale applications. Also been featured in Forbes twice, which is still a little surreal.
