I build AI systems that can critique their own reasoning before humans need to.

Hi, I'm Mandar — a machine learning engineer and researcher working on large-scale AI systems and reasoning-based language models.

My work sits at the intersection of production-scale machine learning and research on explainable reasoning systems. I currently work on LLM-powered ranking systems serving billions of daily impressions.

Previously, I built recommendation and speech systems for millions of children at Epic!, demand forecasting models for Levi Strauss, and traffic simulation environments for autonomous vehicles at NIO.

My research asks a simple question:

How can AI systems critique their own reasoning before humans need to?

I study methods such as self-questioning agents, debate-based reasoning, and reinforcement learning from model critique to build more reliable language models.

I recently published Counterfactual Self-Questioning (CSQ) — a framework where a language model critiques its own reasoning by generating counterfactual alternatives.

The approach integrates with GRPO-style optimization and improves reasoning performance by +6.7 to +12.4 points over chain-of-thought on GSM8K, MATH, and Minerva-style benchmarks.

I also advise (pro bono) on AI strategy for the Western Cape Government in South Africa, helping design a multi-agent platform that scales from internal government use (~10K users) to public-facing services for over 100K citizens.

In 2026 I received the Global Recognition Award for contributions to AI systems deployed in public-sector and large-scale consumer environments.
